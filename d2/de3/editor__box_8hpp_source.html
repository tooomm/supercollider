43 /*
44 A CodeEditorBox represents an IDE document split view: it contains a stack
45 (history) of documents displayed in that split, and an editor widget for each
46 of those documents. Using the document tabs, or the "Switch Document" action
47 one is effectively changing which of those editors is displayed, and thus
48 reordering the viewing history of the CodeEditorBox (the last displayed always
49 moves on top of the stack).
50 
51 A CodeEditorBox becomes "active" when any of its editors receives focus, but
52 it does *not* become inactive when its editors loose focus: it only becomes
53 inactive when another EditorBox becomes active. That allows a CodeEditorBox to
54 be active even if no editor widget in the IDE has focus.
55 
56 The purpose of the "active" CodeEditorBox is for the IDE to direct certain
57 actions to it: e.g. it is the one where newly created and opened documents will
58 be displayed.
59 */
